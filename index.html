How it works

From submission to permanent record.

01 — SUBMIT

See something wrong?

A headline that inverts history. An AI output that launders a false claim. A viral narrative with no source. Submit it.

02 — REVIEW

Human triage.

Reviewers evaluate each submission for source quality, investigability, and evidentiary value. Not every claim becomes a case.

03 — INVESTIGATE

Primary sources. Named actors. Locked chronology.

Approved cases are developed with full evidentiary documentation and Machloket Protocol analysis.

04 — ARCHIVE

Permanent public record.

Verified cases enter the Opposition Library. Documented. Citable. Searchable. The record does not disappear.

05 — RECOGNIZE

Quality rewarded.

Contributors who submit high-quality, verifiable claims are recognized. We reward what builds the archive — not what fills it.

The Forensic AI System

When AI gets Jewish history wrong, we need a system that pushes back.

HevrutAI applies the ancient logic of structured Jewish disagreement to the modern problem of AI-generated misinformation.

What It Does

A human-supervised evidence archive built for a specific problem.

AI systems confidently produce misinformation about Jewish history, Israel, and geopolitics at scale. Communities encounter it in classrooms, family conversations, and social media.

HevrutAI documents these failures. Users submit suspicious claims. Human reviewers investigate using primary sources, chronology analysis, and actor identification. Cases that pass review enter the Opposition Library as permanent public records.

The AI tool supports the process. It does not replace human judgment.

The evidence is the authority.

The Machloket Protocol

Structured disagreement as verification.

Machloket — structured disagreement — is foundational in Jewish legal reasoning. The Talmud preserves minority opinions because future generations may need them.

The protocol runs 28 structured objections against every case: passive voice patterns, false equivalencies, chronological inversions, source credibility laundering, institutional sycophancy signals.

About HevrutAI

The project. The person. The arc.

HevrutAI did not begin as a forensic auditing system. It began as a question about sacred partnership — and deepened into something more urgent.

The Founder

Stacy La Mell

Stacy La Mell is the founder of HevrutAI. She is an educator, visual artist, and curriculum designer who came to AI work through an unexpected path — one that runs through Jewish memory, sacred text, and a deep conviction that how we transmit truth matters as much as what we transmit.

Based in the Chicago area. Available for presentations, partnerships, and consulting engagements.

In 2002, she completed her senior thesis at Evergreen State College — an early rigorous engagement with Jewish identity, the politics of representation, and who gets to tell which stories. As part of that practice, she created the Peace Talks Table — a sculptural installation incorporating barbed wire. Peace and violence sharing the same surface. It is the earliest visual articulation of the adversarial dynamic that became HevrutAI's core methodology.

The Two Eras

How HevrutAI evolved.

ERA ONE — THE COVENANT FRAMEWORK

Sacred partnership as a model for AI

HevrutAI began as a framework for thinking about the human-AI relationship through the lens of hevruta — ancient Jewish paired learning built on rigorous, honest disagreement.

ERA TWO — THE FORENSIC SYSTEM

When the problem became impossible to ignore

AI was producing confident misinformation about Jewish history and Israel at scale. The transmission mission deepened into something urgent. The forensic auditing system grew directly from the earlier vision. Hevruta applied to verification. Machloket applied to truth-seeking. The two eras are the same project, deepened by necessity.

Holocaust Memory Project

The chain almost broke.

It held. And that survival — and the obligation it creates — is the reason HevrutAI exists.

We do not inherit the stories by accident.

We inherit them by commitment. HevrutAI was built in the shadow of testimony — the family whose story grounds this project survived the Shoah and lived to see another generation.

Germany, postwar. From right to left: Nachum Lewin, Max Lewin, and Shmuel Uchin, reunited after surviving the Shoah.

Stacy is named after Shmuel ("Sam") according to Jewish naming tradition.

Living Testimony
"I never thought I would live to see another generation of Jews."
— NACHUM LEWIN

While escaping the ghetto, there were moments when survival itself felt impossible. At one point, Nachum survived on bread mixed with soap while hiding and running.

The Shoah was not only an attack on Jewish bodies. It was an attempted catastrophe of transmission — an effort to ensure there would be no one left to pass anything forward. The people in this photograph survived to be part of the chain continuing.

The chain almost broke. And held.

USC SHOAH FOUNDATION

This family's testimony is preserved in the USC Shoah Foundation archive. Recorded so it cannot be lost. Preserved so it cannot be denied.

Why This Grounds the Forensic Work

In an age when AI can generate plausible falsehoods about Jewish history faster than communities can respond, transmission is not just a spiritual value. It is an urgent practical one.

The HevrutAI forensic auditing system exists because the obligation of testimony is not completed by recording it — it is completed by defending it. Every AI-generated distortion of Jewish history is a small act of erasure. The Machloket Protocol is the response.

The forensic work and the covenantal work are the same work. The only question is which door you came through.
